# General Interview Prompts

Use STAR (Situation, Task, Action, Result) framing and quantify impact.

## Core Prompts

### 1. Walk Us Through Your Project
<details>
<summary>What to highlight.</summary>

- Business context & scale (requests/sec, data volume).
- Your ownership: infra design, automation, on-call.
- Key technical stack and why it was chosen.
- Outcomes (reduced MTTR by 40%, cut cloud spend 15%).
</details>

### 2. Recent Production Incident
<details>
<summary>Structure answer.</summary>

- Trigger (e.g., misconfigured PodDisruptionBudget during upgrade).
- Detection (alerts, customer tickets).
- Mitigation steps.
- Long-term fix (runbooks, tooling, guardrails).
</details>

### 3. Team Contribution (>5 years)
<details>
<summary>Example response.</summary>

Led platform reliability guild, rolled out chaos experiments, mentored junior SREs, implemented feature flag governance, and negotiated error budget policy with product leads.
</details>

### 4. DevOps Experience Summary
<details>
<summary>Key points.</summary>

- Span of responsibilities (CI/CD, infra, observability, incident response).
- Tooling you standardized (Argo CD, Terraform, Datadog).
- Cultural initiatives (blameless postmortems, runbook reviews).
</details>

### 5. Contribution (<5 years)
<details>
<summary>Frame for mid-level engineers.</summary>

Describe automations you shipped (e.g., Helm chart generator), on-call rotations you joined, and the measurable improvements you drove.
</details>

## Interview Tips

- Keep answers <3 minutes, invite follow-ups.
- Tie tech decisions to customer impact or reliability metrics.
- Always mention lessons learned and follow-up actions.
